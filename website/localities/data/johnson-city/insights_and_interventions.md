# Insights and Interventions

## Source-backed insights

1. Bed capacity pressure is quantifiable: 538 unsheltered people vs 407 ES/TH beds (shortfall 131). (`S18`)
2. Throughput pressure appears larger than intake pressure: 1,737 CE enrollments vs 55 literally homeless clients rapidly rehoused. (`S18`)
3. Outreach demand remains sustained across 26 monthly records from 2024-01 through 2026-02. (`S24`)
4. Governance levers are active and documented (funding, HMIS fees, ESG allocation, PIT after-action). (`S20`)
5. Partner activation data exists in attendance exports (59 attendees in one training cycle). (`S22`)

## Software interventions

1. De-identified CE->RRH queue service using `ce_rrh_bed_pressure_view.schema.json`.
2. Portable referral packet exchange using `portable_referral_packet.schema.json`.
3. Automated policy export generation using `policy_export_spec.json` and CSV templates.
4. Monthly outreach scorecard service from `outreach_monthly_records_2024-01_to_2026-02.json`.

## Non-software interventions

1. Set monthly CE->RRH review meetings tied to queue metrics and transition times.
2. Formalize a cross-provider document recovery workflow for missing ID/SSN/birth records.
3. Use ESG participation evidence exports to reduce manual scoring prep burden for partners.
4. Add recurring public de-identified accountability updates to improve trust and visibility.
