When Review Is Required

  • Changes to mission scope, beneficiary groups, or major program design.
  • New data uses, new high-risk data processing, or major privacy/security control changes.
  • Material contractual commitments, partnerships, or resource allocations.
  • Changes that could affect safeguarding, non-discrimination, or legal/regulatory obligations.

Decision Workflow

  1. Prepare a written decision brief with purpose, alternatives, risks, and expected outcomes.
  2. Collect conflict-of-interest disclosures from participants in the decision.
  3. Complete governance review (program, legal/compliance, financial/operational, and safeguarding impact).
  4. Record decision outcome and rationale in the decision log.
  5. Publish policy-facing changes through the public documentation pages, when applicable.

Meeting Model and Cadence

  • Board meetings are convened as needed when there is a decision to make or an issue to review.
  • When permitted by governing documents and applicable law, actions may be approved by written consent instead of a meeting.
  • At minimum, governance records and core policies are reviewed at least annually.
  • If the corporation has voting members, member-meeting requirements are governed by the bylaws and applicable law.
